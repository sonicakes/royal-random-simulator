Jack started cracking on day four. I had him writing at the desk from 6am every morning, keeping his moodlets green, but the moment Wendy tried to chat to him while he was mid-sentence he snapped and stormed off to the hotel bar. That was the beginning of the end.

By week two I'd stopped trying to keep the family happy as a unit and just let things deteriorate. Danny kept wandering the corridors on his own — I never directed him, just watched. He ended up in Room 237 twice. Both times I cancelled the interaction at the last second.

The escape sequence in week three was genuinely tense. Wendy had a fear moodlet I hadn't seen before. I got her and Danny out through the kitchen — Jack was still at the typewriter when the lot reset. I'm counting that as a win.

**What I'd do differently:** Give Jack the Erratic trait instead of Hot-Headed. The random outbursts feel more accurate to the source material, and the unpredictability makes the playthrough feel less like you're managing a slider and more like you're watching something fall apart.
